Project Report 2026

MediScan AI

Refining Clinical Decision Support with Intelligent Vision and Pharmaceutical Context

01 Executive Summary

MediScan AI is a sophisticated clinical decision support tool designed to bridge the gap between complex medical imaging and actionable clinical insights. By leveraging advanced computer vision and localized natural language processing, the system provides instant, secondary diagnostic opinions that prioritize patient safety and clinical accuracy.

02 Problem Statement

Diagnostic Latency

Traditional diagnostic workflows often involve significant delays in report generation, which can be critical in emergency scenarios like strokes or fractures.

Accessibility Gap

Complex medical reports are often inaccessible to patients due to technical jargon and language barriers, particularly in non-English speaking regions.

Prescription Errors

Handwritten prescriptions contribute to significant medication errors. There is a lack of automated tools to cross-check dosages and drug interactions at the point of care.

04 Deep Technical Justification

Resilient OCR Architecture

Unlike standard OCR, our pipeline is designed for High-Noise Medical Environments:

  • Sauvola Local Thresholding: Handles uneven lighting and shadows on physical paper.
  • NLM Denoising: Non-local means filtering preserves delicate handwritten strokes while removing camera sensor noise.
  • Consensus Voting: Aggregates results from multiple Tesseract PSMs (3, 6, 11) using a token-overlap scoring algorithm.

Explainable AI (XAI)

We avoid the "Black Box" problem in clinical settings:

  • Feature Heuristics: Modality detection uses edge density and aspect ratio, ensuring transparency in detection logic.
  • Differential Diagnosis: Always provides secondary possibilities, mimicking the professional diagnostic workflow.
  • ROI Projection: Heatmaps are projected based on clinical findings, not random probability clusters.

Privacy by Design

Compliant with HIPAA & GDPR principles:

  • Local Compute: All PII (Patient Identifiable Information) remains on the local server.
  • Zero-Cloud Dependency: Diagnostic logic is self-contained, preventing external data leaks.
  • SQLite Encapsulation: History and Knowledge base are stored in a portable, flat-file database format.

Clinical Safety Logic

Prioritizing Patient Outcomes:

  • Latin Parsing: Automatically translates "OD/BD/HS" into patient-friendly timing schedules.
  • Interaction Cross-Check: Real-time lookup against a 50+ list of high-risk drug combinations (e.g., Warfarin + Aspirin).
  • Pregnancy Categorization: FDA safety metadata integration for all searched medicines.

05 Architectural Choices

Frontend Philosophy Performance-First Vanilla JS
Backend Choice Python (Clinical Standard)
Data Privacy Local Processing (PII Safe)
Accessibility Bi-lingual (En/Ta) + Tanglish

The choice of Vanilla JS over heavy frameworks ensures the application remains snappy on low-power hospital terminals. The Python backend allows for seamless integration with medical imaging libraries while maintaining a clean, RESTful API.
